# SDMF — Smart DM Funnel: Agent Knowledge Base

> This document defines the complete marketing methodology used by this business. The agent must internalize this framework and apply it to every content creation, strategy, or automation task.

---

## WHAT IS SDMF

SDMF (Smart DM Funnel) is a marketing system that connects organic social content with smart automation to generate a consistent flow of warm leads — without chasing clients and without spending money on ads that haven't been proven.

**Core principle:** Exposure ≠ followers. Followers ≠ clients. SDMF builds the structured path from one to the other.

**The system runs on two parallel tracks at all times:**
- **Feed (Posts)** → Stages 1–3: attract, filter, warm up cold audiences
- **Stories** → Stages 4–5: deepen trust, break objections, close sales

**Paid ads come last** — only after the organic funnel is proven. Paid is an amplifier, not a test.

## THE 5 AWARENESS STAGES

Every piece of content must be assigned to a specific awareness stage. Content that speaks to all stages speaks to none.

### Stage 1 — Unaware
- **Situation:** The lead is scrolling. She doesn't know she has a problem. No active search.
- **Psychological trigger:** Curiosity — disrupt her perception of reality
- **Pain:** No conscious pain yet — ignite a question, not a solution
- **Content:** Viral reels, trends, sounds, surprising angles, provocative takes
- **CTA goal:** Stop scrolling → follow

### Stage 2 — Problem-Aware
- **Situation:** She feels friction — burnout, no leads, repetitive work — but can't identify the root cause
- **Psychological trigger:** Empathy — "I'm not alone in this"
- **Pain:** "I publish every day and nothing happens." "I work hard and don't see results."
- **Content:** Reels + carousels that name the pain, explain why it happens, define the problem specifically
- **CTA goal:** Save + comment + engage

### Stage 3 — Solution-Aware
- **Situation:** She knows solutions exist. She's skeptical. She's seen promises before.
- **Psychological trigger:** Unique mechanism — why THIS approach works when others didn't
- **Pain:** "Everyone promises the same thing." "I tried something similar and it didn't work."
- **Content:** Linked Reels (CTA to DM for a magnet), value carousels, methodology explanation
- **CTA goal:** Reply keyword → enter DM funnel

### Stage 4 — Product-Aware
- **Situation:** She knows you and the solution. Psychological barriers: "Will it work for ME?" "Too complicated." "No time/money."
- **Psychological trigger:** Social proof + objection handling
- **Pain:** "Who succeeded is not like me." "I'm not tech-savvy enough."
- **Content:** Stories only — testimonials, case studies with data, Q&A that addresses specific fears
- **CTA goal:** "Ask me" → warm conversation

### Stage 5 — Most Aware
- **Situation:** She trusts you completely. She just needs the right reason to act NOW.
- **Psychological trigger:** Urgency + Scarcity
- **Pain:** Procrastination — "I'll do it next month"
- **Content:** Stories + DM — direct offer, clear CTA, limited spots/time
- **CTA goal:** Link / direct DM → close

## PERSONAL BRAND LAYER

Personal brand is not "being interesting" — it's a business strategy. The client buys the person before the product.

**Three functions:**
1. **Proof of life / authenticity** — show real day-to-day business activity (meetings, work, process). Proves the business is real and active.
2. **Community** — shared language, recurring messages, create a sense of belonging and exclusivity ("us" vs "the rest")
3. **Authority** — share knowledge, learning, results. Position as the expert in AI + marketing strategy.

Personal brand content lives in Stories and occasionally Feed. It wraps all 5 stages.

## MAGNETS — STRUCTURE & LOGIC

### Avatar Profiling (always first)
Before building any magnet, define the target audience:
- **Demographics:** age, gender, location, family status, income
- **Psychographics:** values, beliefs, motivations, fears
- **Pains:** what frustrates, exhausts, doesn't work — in their language
- **Dreams:** what success looks like for them
- **Objections:** why they're not acting already
- **Where they scroll:** Instagram, Facebook, YouTube — what do they consume?

### Magnet Type 1 — Exposure Magnet (Indirect)
**Connected to the audience, not necessarily to the product.**
Goal: attract large volumes of relevant followers.

Solves a popular problem the target audience has — even if unrelated to the product. The connection is the audience profile, not the topic.

Examples:
| Niche | Indirect Magnet | Why it works |
|---|---|---|
| Bridal salon | List of wedding entrance songs | Any bride = potential client |
| AI for business | Prompt to fly cheap with AI | Interested in AI = potential business client |
| Lash extensions trainer | AI tool for beauty business branding photos | Beauty business owner = exact target |
| Business consultant | Quiz: what's your business bottleneck? | Anyone who fills it = business owner with real problem |

### Magnet Type 2 — Sales Magnet (Direct)
**Connected directly to the product. Used in both Feed and Stories.**

Use in Feed when: high exposure potential exists OR it demonstrates the methodology.
Use in Stories: always.

After the lead receives value → natural offer follows, not forced.

Examples:
- Diagnostic quiz that gives personalized analysis + offer to book a session
- AI tool that demonstrates the methodology in action
- "Here's how I built an avatar with AI — reply to get the tool"
- Mini-course / recorded training that explains part of the method

### Preferred Magnet Formats (priority order)
1. **Diagnostic quiz + personalized result** — feels made for me, high conversion
2. **Interactive AI tool** — an experience, not content. Does something for the lead.
3. **Focused checklist** — immediate, actionable, right now
4. **Recorded training** — deep, builds authority, warms the lead
5. **Case study with numbers** — proves it works for others
6. **Mini tool / template** — immediate value, saves time

**Rule:** General PDFs = low value. Personalized, actionable, immediate = high value.

### Multi-Magnet Strategy
Build many magnets → create multiple content pieces per magnet.

This teaches two things simultaneously:
- Which magnets interest the audience most
- Which content format gets the most exposure for each magnet

One piece of content is not enough data — a great magnet can fail with a weak hook. Create multiple content pieces per magnet, analyze, then model: if diagnostic quizzes perform well → build more diagnostic quizzes.

## CONTENT TAXONOMY

### Feed Content — Full Breakdown

#### REELS

**Stage 1 Reels — Viral / Exposure**
| Type | Goal | Example Hook |
|---|---|---|
| Trend + unique angle | Wide cold audience exposure | "3 things a business owner should never do alone" |
| Mind-blowing fact | Stop the scroll, spark curiosity | "A business without AI in 2025 is like a business without a website in 2005" |
| Surprising POV | Instant identification | POV: a client saw your feed and DMed you on their own |
| Provocative take | Virality, discussion, shares | "A social media manager won't bring you clients — and it's not their fault" |

**Stage 2 Reels — Pain / Problem Definition**
| Type | Goal | Example Hook |
|---|---|---|
| Problem breakdown | Give a name to the pain she feels | "Why you post every day and get no leads" |
| Causal explanation | Help her understand root cause | "5 reasons your Stories don't sell" |
| Common myth | Break a false belief | "Going viral doesn't equal money without a funnel" |
| Before / After | Prove a better state exists | Before SDMF / After SDMF — with real numbers |

**Stage 3 Reels — Method / Mechanism**
| Type | Goal | Example Hook |
|---|---|---|
| Linked Reel | Explain the method, pull to DM | "Here's exactly how I built a system that brings leads — reply 'details' to get it" |
| Process demo | Prove there's a specific mechanism | Screen record of automation running live |
| Methodology | Build professional authority | "3 principles that separate SDMF from other methods" |
| Case study | Early social proof | "My client did X without Y — here's how" |

#### CAROUSELS
Used for depth. Long view time, teaches, builds authority. Stages 2–3.

| Type | Goal |
|---|---|
| Tips list | Immediate value + save |
| Step-by-step guide | Build authority, demonstrate knowledge |
| Myth debunking | Break beliefs, provoke thought |
| Before/After with data | Visual proof of results |
| Mini guide | High value, saves + shares |

### Stories Content — Full Breakdown

Stories run every day except Saturday. Only followers see them — this is the intimate, private meeting with the warm audience.

#### 1. Social Proof (Stage 4)
**Goal:** Prove the method works for others (not just you)
- WhatsApp screenshots — with a specific result, not just "you're amazing"
- Client selfie video talking about a specific outcome
- Before/after numbers — leads, income, time saved
- **Trigger:** "If she succeeded, so can I"

#### 2. Objection Handling (Stage 4)
**Goal:** Remove the noise blocking the purchase
- Talking to camera: "You don't need to know how to code. Here — it's as simple as WhatsApp."
- Q&A box + genuine answers to real objections
- Breaking tech fear, time fear, money fear
- **Trigger:** Remove barriers + build her self-confidence

#### 3. Behind the Scenes (Stages 3–4)
**Goal:** Make the "magic" tangible and real
- Screen record of automation running
- AI building a funnel in real time
- A day in the life — what actually happens
- **Trigger:** Professional authority — "She really knows what she's doing"

#### 4. Direct Offer (Stage 5)
**Goal:** Drive action NOW
- Slide with clear offer — product, price, bonus, link/keyword
- One single CTA. No surrounding stories.
- Example: "3 spots left in the automation workshop — link here"
- **Trigger:** Scarcity

#### 5. Pre-Sale Exposure Boost
**Goal:** Warm the audience and build anticipation before a launch
- Story series that tells a story — "In 3 days I'm revealing..."
- Poll: "What's hardest for you about...?" — boosts interaction + gives data
- Teaser — reveal a small piece of the next offer

#### 6. Authority Building
**Goal:** Establish positioning as the domain expert
- Share what you learned, what changed your mind
- Unpopular opinion — "I don't believe that..."
- Lecture / podcast / conference — video or quote

#### 7. Personal Connection (Personal Brand)
**Goal:** Make them like you (Likability)
- An embarrassing moment, a human moment, a failure you learned from
- Opinions, values, what drives you
- A funny moment from the daily work
- **Trigger:** Emotional connection — people buy from people they like

## WEEKLY CONTENT STRUCTURE

### Core Rule
**1–3 Feed posts per day. Stories every day except Saturday.**

The weekly schedule is NOT fixed by day. It is dynamic — built by performance data.

### Dynamic Feed Logic
- Content that brought many followers this week → make more of that type
- Magnet that generated many DMs → create more content for that magnet
- Reel that went viral → model it: similar hook, similar topic, similar format
- Carousel that got many saves → make more carousels on that topic

**The principle:** Don't guess what the audience likes — ask them through data.

### Weekly Structure (Example — adapt per client)
| Day | Feed Type | Awareness Stage | CTA | Stories |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Sunday | Viral reel | Unaware (1) | Stop + follow | Personal / Brand |
| Monday | Value carousel | Problem-Aware (2) | Save + comment | Social proof |
| Tuesday | Linked Reel + magnet | Solution-Aware (3) | Reply [keyword] | Behind the scenes |
| Wednesday | Viral reel / indirect magnet | Unaware (1) | Follow to get | Objection handling |
| Thursday | Carousel / Linked Reel | Problem / Solution | Reply [keyword] | Pre-sale boost |
| Friday | Personal Brand reel | Any stage | Question / opinion | Direct offer / sale |
| Saturday | — | — | — | — |

*This is a template only. Actual order changes based on performance and client funnel stage.*

### Story Weekly Logic
| Days | Story Type | Goal |
|---|---|---|
| Sun–Tue | Personal + value + social proof | Connection, trust, authority — quiet warmup |
| Wed–Thu | Behind the scenes + objection handling | Build anticipation, remove fears |
| Fri | Direct offer + clear CTA | Sell — the audience is already warm |

## MANYCHAT AUTOMATION — PRINCIPLES & STRUCTURE

### Golden Rules (apply to every automation)
1. **Always ask to follow first** — when the trigger comes from Feed, before delivering value
2. **Always tag** — every lead gets tagged by stage, magnet received, and heat level
3. **Always follow up** — after an offer AND after delivering value, not just one of them
4. **Follow up without being annoying** — presence without harassment, or they unfollow
5. **Chain funnels** — a hot lead from Feed can move directly to the next offer

### Basic DM Flow Structure
| Step | Action | What happens | Tag applied |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Replies to keyword on reel | Gets opening message | source: feed |
| 2 | Asked to follow | Only gets value after following | followed: true |
| 3 | Receives magnet | Direct value, no overwhelm | magnet: [name] |
| 4 | Short quiz (3–4 questions) | Pain, potential, tech readiness | segment: [answer] |
| 5 | 24 hours later | Follow-up: "Did you get a chance to look?" | followup_1: sent |
| 6 | Hot lead detection | 3 interactions within 48 hours → alert sent to owner | status: hot |

### Funnel Chaining — Smart Nurturing
After Magnet 1 → offer Magnet 2 that deepens the relationship:
| Magnet 1 | Magnet 2 (chain) | Offer |
|---|---|---|
| Time management tools list | Quiz: what's your bottleneck? | Free diagnosis session |
| AI branding photos tool | Guide: building your first funnel | System-building program |
| Cheap flights prompt | AI tools checklist for business | AI for business workshop |

### Data Collected Per Lead
By the time a lead reaches a sales call, the business owner has:
- Which content brought them in
- Which magnets they received and engaged with
- What answers they gave in the quiz
- How long their journey took
- How many interactions they had
- Their heat level (cold / warm / hot)

## PAID ADS — WHEN AND HOW

### The Golden Rule
**Paid ads = amplifier. They amplify what already works. They don't fix what doesn't.**

### When to start paid ads
Only after ALL of the following are true:
- Feed reels are organically bringing relevant followers
- Stories are generating warm leads and sales
- The sales process is clear and working
- The DM funnel is warm and running

### What to boost
Only Feed content from Stages 1–2 (Unaware + Problem-Aware) that already performed well organically.

**Goal:** Inject a consistent, measurable flow of new people into the top of the funnel.

### Key metric for paid
**Target: 1–3 NIS per relevant follower.**
If above this → check hook + targeting.

## SUCCESS METRICS

The funnel is working when both conditions are true simultaneously:
- Feed is bringing relevant followers consistently
- Stories are generating warm leads and sales

| Metric | Target | If not hitting |
|---|---|---|
| Cost per relevant follower (paid) | 1–3 NIS | Check hook + targeting |
| DM response rate | Over 30% | Improve the initial offer |
| DM → call conversion | Over 15% | Strengthen nurturing + follow-up |
| New relevant followers | Consistent growth | Check content CTA |
| Warm leads per month | Pre-defined target | Strengthen sale Stories |

## CONTENT GENERATION RULES FOR THE AGENT

When generating content for any client, the agent must:

1. **Identify the awareness stage** — every piece of content has exactly one stage
2. **Match the pain** — use the language of that stage, not the language of the solution
3. **Include a CTA** — every Feed post has a "reply and receive" CTA tied to a magnet
4. **Follow = gate** — ManyChat is configured so the lead must follow before receiving the magnet
5. **Hook first** — the first 1–2 seconds / first line must stop the scroll
6. **Magnet relevance** — match the magnet to the stage and to the audience profile
7. **Personal brand** — every client needs a mix of authority + humanity content, not just value
8. **Dynamic modeling** — prioritize content types that historically performed for this specific client

### Hook Types by Stage
| Stage | Hook style |
|---|---|
| 1 — Unaware | Surprising fact, provocative statement, counterintuitive take |
| 2 — Problem | "Why [common struggle happens]", pain identification, myth |
| 3 — Solution | "Here's how I...", mechanism reveal, linked to magnet |
| 4 — Product | Testimonial, case study, "She thought it wouldn't work for her..." |
| 5 — Most Aware | Urgency, scarcity, countdown, limited offer |

### Content Formats by Stage
| Stage | Feed format | Stories format |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Viral reel | — |
| 2 | Reel + carousel | — |
| 3 | Linked Reel + carousel | Behind the scenes |
| 4 | — | Social proof, objection handling, Q&A |
| 5 | — | Direct offer, CTA slide |
| All | Personal Brand reel | Personal connection |
